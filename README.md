# Notice of Code Withdrawal

## Why my repositories have been removed

I have chosen to withdraw my code from GitHub in protest of **[California's Digital Age Assurance Act (AB 1043)](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB1043)** and **[Colorado's SB26-051](https://leg.colorado.gov/bills/SB26-051)** — legislation set to take effect **January 1, 2027** — which would require operating system vendors to include an **age verification or age logging mechanism** in their software.

These laws are, in my view, both technically misguided and deeply troubling from a privacy and civil-liberties standpoint. Mandating that an operating system track or verify the age of its users introduces surveillance infrastructure at the most fundamental layer of personal computing. Such a requirement:

- **Violates user privacy** by compelling the collection of sensitive personal data at the OS level.
- **Creates security risks** by building identity-logging mechanisms into software that is trusted with full access to a user's hardware and data.
- **Sets a dangerous precedent** for government-mandated backdoors and tracking in general-purpose software.
- **Burdens open-source developers** with compliance obligations that are impractical or impossible to implement without compromising the principles of free and open software.

I refuse to have my work hosted on a platform subject to such a legal environment while these laws are on the books — even before they take effect on January 1, 2027. My code will not be used as any part of a chain that enables this kind of surveillance infrastructure.

## What this means

All of my public repositories have been removed or made private. If you were depending on any of my projects, I apologize for the disruption.

## When I will return

I will reconsider my position if:

1. These laws are repealed or struck down by the courts.
2. A meaningful exemption is established for open-source and privacy-respecting software.

It would also help if the legislators responsible for these bills demonstrated a basic understanding of the technology they are legislating — the kind of understanding that most people their age apparently rely on their grandchildren to explain when trying to connect a new personal electronic device to WiFi.

Until then, my code will not be publicly available on this platform.
